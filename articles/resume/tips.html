Resume Tips

Here are some tips for building your resume. These are paraphrased from Gayle Laakmann McDowell's famous Cracking the Coding Interview.

  • Keep the length to 1 page or less. If you have decades of experience 1 - 2 pages is acceptable.
  • Only include work history relevant to the position you are applying for, things that make you seem more impressive.
  • For each bullet point discuss your accomplishments during your employment with the general format "Accomplished X by implementing Y which led to Z."
  • Include 2-4 of your most significant projects. State what the project was and what languages/technologies you used.
  • Independent/projects done in your personal time are more impressive than projects done for a class.
  • Don't add too many projects/small or insignificant projects.
  • You can list your skills/languages you know but be careful because listing those things makes them open game for interview topics. You may want to list your level of proficiency next to each skill/language.
